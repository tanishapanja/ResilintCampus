Earthquake

Here are the top 5 precautions for an earthquake in simple lines-

1. Drop, Cover, and Hold On during the shaking.

2. Stay away from windows, glass, and heavy objects.

3. Keep emergency supplies ready (water, food, first-aid, torch).

4. Secure heavy furniture to walls to prevent falling.

5. Move to an open area if you are outside, away from buildings and trees.

Fire Safety

How To use A fire extinguisher-

1. Pull the safety pin to unlock the extinguisher.

2. Aim the nozzle at the base of the fire (not the flames).

3. Squeeze the handle to release the extinguishing agent.

Check if the fire is out and be ready to repeat if needed.

Cyclone/Flood

How to be safe during Cyclone -

1.Stay indoors and away from windows during a cyclone.

2.Keep emergency supplies like food, water, and a flashlight ready

3.Don't go for fish catching or swimming

4.Follow official warnings and evacuate if advised.

Landslide

What to do during landslide-

1.Avoid living or traveling in landslide-prone areas, especially during heavy rain.

2.Stay alert for warning signs like cracks in the ground or falling rocks.

3.Move to a safe, higher place and follow evacuation instructions if a landslide is likely.

Forest Fire

Understanding perimeter safety and smoke inhalation protection-

1. Avoid lighting fires in forests and never leave flames or cigarettes unattended.

2. If a fire starts, move away quickly in the opposite direction of the wind to a safe, open area.

3. Cover your nose and mouth with a cloth and follow evacuation instructions to stay safe.

Heatwave

Hydration protocols and identifying signs of heat exhaustion-

1.Drink plenty of water and stay hydrated.

2.Stay indoors in a cool or shaded place as much as possible.

3.Avoid going out during peak heat hours (12 PM to 4 PM)
